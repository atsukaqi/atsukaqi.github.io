## Timeline
### History of Chinese New Year
The Timeline shows the origins and development of the Chinese New Year. As China's most important traditional festival, the Chinese New Year has a history of thousands of years. The Timeline shows the evolution from ancient sacrificial activities based on a farming culture to modern celebrations, including traditional customs such as family reunions, firecrackers and spring couplets.
In modern times, especially after the founding of the People's Republic of China(1949), the Chinese New Year was officially established as a national holiday. Traditional and modern elements have merged with each other in the celebration of the Chinese New Year, making it the most important holiday for Chinese people around the world.

## Party Safety
### Original data from [PartyData](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1Hqxr7RJKM4odhVEsrfdzetl47WPrl0UcLxqXjCGU7L8/edit?usp=sharing)
Based on the data and graph, there are noticeable gender differences in students' party preferences and experiences of unsafe conditions. For off-campus house parties, 20.46% of female respondents reported experiencing unsafe conditions, compared to 15.25% of male respondents, indicating that females might be at a slightly higher risk of encountering unsafe situations in these settings.
In fraternity house parties, both males and females reported unsafe experiences, but again, the percentage was higher among females (17.95%) than males (9.46%). Additionally, some students who did not have a specific preference for party location also reported unsafe experiences, though at a lower rate. These statistics suggest that while both genders may encounter safety issues at parties, females tend to report such experiences at a higher rate than males.

## Tableau
### World Happiness Report 2024: Comparative analysis of GDP and social support in the Top 5 and Bottom 5 countries
In modern society, where people are often stressed by life and work, the motivation for this project stems from my curiosity about the factors that influence people's happiness(ladder score). I chose GDP and social support as the focus of my analysis because of their notable role in improving a country's happiness level.

I selected data from the The [World Happiness Report Appendices & Data](https://worldhappiness.report/data/)  (the most recent available) and visualized the GDP and social support scores for the top 5 (e.g., Finland, Denmark, etc.) and bottom 5 (e.g., Afghanistan, Sierra Leone, etc.) countries via Tableau. The project demonstrates the extent to which these two factors influence happiness, helping us to visualize more the differences in happiness across countries.

The analysis shows that the top 5 countries typically have high GDP and strong social support systems; while the bottom 5 countries generally score lower on both. It reveals the polarization of happiness and highlights the key role of economic and social support in enhancing happiness.
